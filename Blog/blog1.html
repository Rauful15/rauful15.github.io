Why Stereochemistry Matters in Drug Discovery

May 12, 2026

Stereochemistry plays a critical role in modern drug discovery. Two molecules may have identical molecular formulas yet behave completely differently inside the human body because of their three-dimensional arrangement.

Enantiomers often interact differently with proteins, enzymes, and receptors because biological systems are inherently chiral. One stereoisomer may show therapeutic activity while another may be inactive or even toxic.

Understanding chirality, stereocenters, and conformational behavior therefore becomes essential for medicinal chemists.
